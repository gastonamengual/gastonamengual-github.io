Logistic Growth is a mathematical function characterized by increasing growth in the beginning period, but a decreasing growth at a later stage, as you get closer to a maximum.

$y(t) = \dfrac{c}{1+a \cdot e^{-bt}}$

In which:

  • y(t) is the number of cases at any given time t
  • c is the limiting value, the maximum capacity for y
  • b has to be larger than 0

Two very other interesting points about this formula are: * The number of cases at the beginning, also called initial value is: $\dfrac{c}{1 + a}$ * The maximum growth rate is at $t = \dfrac{ln(a)}{b}$ and $y(t) = \dfrac{c}{2}$

def logistic_growth(t, a, b, c):
    return c / (1 + a * np.exp(-b * t))
