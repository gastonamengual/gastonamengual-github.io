Logistic Growth

Modeling and Simulation Mon 20 July 2020

Notebook written by Gastón Amengual.

Logistic Growth is a mathematical function characterized by increasing growth in the beginning period, but a decreasing growth at a later stage, as you get closer to a maximum. It is an S-shaped curve with equation

$$y(t) = \dfrac{c}{1+a \cdot e^{-bt}}$$

In which:

  • \(y(t)\) is the number of cases at any given time \(t\)
  • \(c\) is the limiting value, the maximum capacity for \(y\)
  • \(b\) has to be larger than \(0\)

The number of cases at the beginning, also called initial value is: \(\dfrac{c}{1 + a}\)

The maximum growth rate is at \(t = \dfrac{ln(a)}{b}\) and \(y(t) = \dfrac{c}{2}\)

def logistic_growth(t, a, b, c):
    return c / (1 + a * np.exp(-b * t))
